What I Want

Know exactly where I stand and build the infrastructure for the journey — before spending a dollar on review materials.

What It Does

Eliminates guesswork. You don't waste months studying the wrong thing or miss a registration window that sets back your timeline by a quarter.

How to Achieve It
  1. Register on NASBA's CPA Central portal — create your candidate account at nasba.org
  2. Apply to NY State Board of Accountancy via NYSED (op.nysed.gov) — submit transcripts for credit verification
  3. Choose your Discipline section: given Real Estate + advisory interest → BAR (Business Analysis & Reporting) or TCP (Tax Compliance & Planning) for client-facing tax work
  4. Select a review course: Becker (most comprehensive, ~$1,500–$2,000), Roger/UWorld (high-energy lectures), or Ninja (budget-friendly ~$600)
  5. Set up your Bullet Journal CPA spread: one page per section, tracking MCQ scores, study hours, and weak topics weekly

What I Want

Pass the hardest CPA section first, while my accounting coursework from UAlbany is still fresh in memory.

What It Does

FAR covers GAAP, financial statements, governmental accounting, and nonprofits — the backbone of financial literacy. Passing it first builds maximum confidence and eliminates the heaviest lift early. Your 30-month rolling clock to pass all 4 sections starts here.

How to Achieve It
  1. Study 15–20 hrs/week. Break into 4 modules: Financial Statements, Governmental, Nonprofit, Conceptual Framework
  2. Use your review course's adaptive QBank — minimum 30 MCQs per day. Log weak topics weekly in BuJo
  3. Introduce Task-Based Simulations (TBSs) at Week 6 — simulate real exam conditions under time pressure
  4. Target: consistently scoring 70–75%+ on practice exams before scheduling the real test
  5. Schedule at a Prometric center (Albany or NYC). Minimum passing score: 75 out of 99
💡 Financial IQ #1 — Making More Money. FAR is the language of financial reporting — the foundation of reading any business's financial health and the first real credentialing milestone.
What I Want

Pass AUD to prove professional-grade skepticism and process thinking — the mindset of someone who verifies before trusting.

What It Does

AUD tests risk assessment, internal controls evaluation, and ethics — directly applicable to Caceres Agency when advising small businesses on financial systems and controls. This is the section that makes you trustworthy to clients.

How to Achieve It
  1. Study 12–16 hrs/week. Modules: Ethics & Responsibilities, Risk Assessment, Audit Procedures, Reporting
  2. AUD is logic-heavy — for every MCQ you get wrong, write why in your BuJo (not just what the answer was)
  3. Practice the auditor's mindset: What could go wrong? Who is responsible? What evidence exists?
  4. Pair with any ISC discipline prep if that's your chosen section (overlapping SOC/attestation content)
  5. Schedule within 60 days of passing FAR to maintain momentum and protect your 30-month window
💡 Financial IQ #2 — Protecting Your Money. AUD teaches how money is verified and safeguarded — essential for any investor or business owner advising clients on financial integrity.
What I Want

Pass REG to command the tax code — the single most immediately useful section for real life, Caceres Agency, and real estate investing.

What It Does

REG covers federal individual and entity taxation, business law, and ethics. It directly powers your ability to structure LLCs, advise small business clients on tax strategy, and plan real estate investments in NYC and Paute intelligently.

How to Achieve It
  1. Study 12–16 hrs/week. Modules: Individual Tax, Entity Tax (S-Corps, Partnerships, LLCs), Business Law, Ethics
  2. Note: New federal tax provisions may become testable on REG — confirm with your review course that materials are current for your exam window
  3. Connect every concept to real life: map each rule back to Caceres Agency's LLC structure or a real estate deal scenario
  4. Use REG and TCP overlap strategically — study individual taxation content that appears in both sections
  5. Target: Pass REG by Month 7. Three cores complete. Rolling window fully engaged.
💡 Financial IQ #3 & #4 — Budgeting + Leveraging Money. REG is where you learn how the wealthy use the tax code as a wealth-building tool, not just a compliance obligation.
